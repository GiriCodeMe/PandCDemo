06 — Narrative Summary
On March 10, 2026 at 14:22 hours, Miami-Dade Fire Rescue units responded to a reported structure fire at 456 Oak Avenue. Upon arrival, E-36 observed smoke conditions from the kitchen area with no visible external flame. Occupant Mary Johnson had self-evacuated prior to arrival and reported a toaster oven malfunction approximately 10 minutes before calling 911. Fire was contained to the kitchen area with smoke extension to the dining room and living room. Investigation by INV-3 confirmed the area of origin as the kitchen south wall, adjacent to the countertop where the toaster oven was located. Cause confirmed as accidental electrical ignition — short circuit within the appliance. NO accelerant detected during on-scene investigation. Canine unit (K9-2) deployed — negative result. No suspicious indicators identified. STRUCTURAL NOTE: Investigator observed potential load-bearing wall involvement in the kitchen south wall area. MDFR recommends structural engineering assessment prior to re-occupancy. Property deemed UNINHABITABLE pending structural clearance. Insured cooperated fully with investigation. No injuries reported. Red Cross temporary housing resources provided to occupant.
